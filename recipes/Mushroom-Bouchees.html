Mushroom Bouchees

Description

Perfect, bite-size mushroom appetizers that are always a hit at parties and family gatherings alike. Even the little ones love them - just don't tell them they're made with mushrooms! The recipe comes from a French-Canadian grandmother whose kitchen creations are magnifique.

Ingredients

  • Cooking spray
  • 3 Green Onions
  • 1 (10 oz) Can Mushroom Pieces, Drained
  • 2 tbsp. Butter
  • 2 tbsp. Cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup half-and-half
  • Salt and Ground Black Pepper to taste
  • 1 pinch Garlic Powder
  • 10 slices Sandwich Bread

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).Spray a baking sheet with cooking spray.
  2. Chop green onions and mushrooms very finely, to a nearly paste-like consistency.
  3. Melt butter in a small saucepan over medium heat; stir in green onions and mushrooms and cook and stir until fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  4. Whisk cornstarch into half-and-half; pour into mushroom mixture and cook, stirring often, until thickened, about 3 minutes. Season mushroom mixture with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  5. Roll bread slices with a rolling pin until very flat and thin.
  6. Spread mushroom mixture evenly onto flattened bread; tighly roll up bread slices. Slice each bread tube into 1-inch thick rounds, place rounds onto a baking sheet.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven until bread is browned, about 10 minutes.
